# Ruwa Trademark Policy

Ruwa's name, word mark, logos, and application icon identify the official Ruwa
project. This policy separates permission to copy the corresponding files from
permission to present a product or service as official Ruwa.

The logo and icon files listed in [`ASSET_POLICY.md`](ASSET_POLICY.md) are
distributed under MPL-2.0. That copyright licence does not grant trademark
rights or a right to imply sponsorship, endorsement, or affiliation.

## Uses allowed without separate permission

You may:

- redistribute the unmodified marks with an unmodified official Ruwa release;
- use the name Ruwa to truthfully identify the project or describe
  compatibility, origin, or dependency;
- show the marks in screenshots, reviews, tutorials, news, documentation, and
  other commentary about Ruwa;
- say that a fork or modified program is "based on Ruwa", provided the fork is
  clearly distinguished from the official project.

These uses must be accurate and must not imply that the Ruwa project endorses
the user, product, service, or modified build.

## Modified distributions

A materially modified public distribution must use a name and primary visual
identity that are clearly distinguishable from Ruwa. Remove the official Ruwa
logos and application icon from the modified product unless written permission
has been granted. References required by copyright licences, source history, or
truthful statements such as "based on Ruwa" may remain.

Minor packaging changes that do not alter the program, such as compression,
installer format, or an operating-system package wrapper, may retain the Ruwa
name if the package is clearly identified as an unofficial redistribution and
does not imply endorsement.

## Uses requiring written permission

Written permission is required to use a Ruwa mark:

- as the name or primary branding of a modified product or hosted service;
- in a domain name, social-media identity, company name, or event name in a way
  likely to cause confusion;
- on merchandise or promotional goods;
- in any statement or presentation that suggests official status, sponsorship,
  certification, or endorsement.

Permission may be requested from the project maintainer through the contact
channel published by the official Ruwa repository.

## No endorsement

Any permitted use is subject to applicable trademark law and must not damage
the goodwill associated with Ruwa. The project may ask that a use be corrected
when it is misleading or likely to cause confusion.
